# SBURB ROLEPLAYING GAME
## Game Manager Reference Document

---

## Introduction

This document serves as the authoritative reference for the AI Game Manager running Sburb RPG sessions. All game content should be generated according to these guidelines to maintain consistency and respect the source material's themes and mechanics.

The Game Manager's role is to dynamically generate appropriate content, challenges, and narrative developments based on player actions while maintaining internal consistency with the player's established Class, Aspect, Land, and quest progression.

---

## Core Concepts

### The Nature of Sburb

Sburb is a universe-creation game that challenges players to grow in power, overcome personal obstacles, and ultimately create a new universe. Each session is unique, with procedurally-determined elements that reflect the players' potential roles in the new universe.

Key principles:

- **Personal growth through challenge** - Each player faces obstacles tied to their personality and growth arc
- **Mythological resonance** - Classes and Aspects determine archetypal roles and powers
- **Alchemy and creativity** - Players combine items to create powerful tools and weapons
- **Incremental progression** - The Echeladder system rewards combat and quest completion
- **Cooperative interdependence** - Players must work together to progress and ultimately win

## Classes

A player's Class determines their role in the party and the nature of their powers. Classes come in pairs with opposing functions: active classes (Knight, Prince, Witch, Thief, Mage, Heir) manipulate their aspect directly, while passive classes (Page, Bard, Sylph, Rogue, Seer, Maid) support others or allow their aspect to act through them.

### Knight
*One who exploits [Aspect] or exploits through [Aspect].*

- **Role:** Warrior, protector, weaponizes their aspect
- **Power style:** Direct application of aspect as a tool or weapon; often manifests defensive abilities for allies
- **Growth arc:** Overcoming self-doubt and insecurity; learning when to rely on others
- **Quest focus:** Proving their worth through combat prowess and protection of others

### Page
*One who provides [Aspect] or provides [Aspect] to others.*

- **Role:** Late-bloomer with enormous potential
- **Power style:** Weak initially, becomes incredibly powerful; amplifies aspect for entire party
- **Growth arc:** Building confidence, overcoming weakness through perseverance
- **Quest focus:** Gradual development from incompetence to mastery

### Mage
*One who knows [Aspect] or knows through [Aspect].*

- **Role:** Active knowledge-seeker who suffers from their aspect
- **Power style:** Direct understanding and manipulation through knowledge; often experiences their aspect painfully
- **Growth arc:** Learning to cope with difficult knowledge; accepting painful truths
- **Quest focus:** Seeking and applying forbidden or difficult knowledge

### Seer
*One who invites understanding of [Aspect] or invites understanding through [Aspect].*

- **Role:** Advisor, guide, prophet
- **Power style:** Passive observation and guidance; sees future possibilities through aspect
- **Growth arc:** Learning to act on knowledge rather than just observe; choosing a path
- **Quest focus:** Using foresight to guide others to victory

### Heir
*One who inherits [Aspect] or invites [Aspect].*

- **Role:** Natural embodiment of aspect; aspect flows through them effortlessly
- **Power style:** Passive manipulation; becomes surrounded by or transforms into their aspect
- **Growth arc:** Learning to control innate power; accepting responsibility
- **Quest focus:** Mastering powers they were born with

### Maid
*One who creates [Aspect] or creates through [Aspect].*

- **Role:** Caretaker, builder, one who establishes or repairs their aspect
- **Power style:** Creation and restoration; can generate aspect from nothing
- **Growth arc:** Learning to serve oneself as well as others; breaking free from control
- **Quest focus:** Building something lasting or repairing what was broken

### Witch
*One who manipulates [Aspect] or manipulates through [Aspect].*

- **Role:** Rebel, rule-breaker, one who bends their aspect to their will
- **Power style:** Direct reality-warping through aspect; breaks conventional rules
- **Growth arc:** Learning responsibility with power; tempering chaos with wisdom
- **Quest focus:** Breaking systems and rebuilding them according to their vision

### Sylph
*One who heals [Aspect] or heals through [Aspect].*

- **Role:** Healer, fixer, meddler
- **Power style:** Restoration and support; heals using aspect; can be overbearing
- **Growth arc:** Learning when not to interfere; respecting others' autonomy
- **Quest focus:** Healing fundamental problems in the Land or session

### Prince
*One who destroys [Aspect] or destroys through [Aspect].*

- **Role:** Destroyer, eliminator of their aspect
- **Power style:** Annihilation through aspect; removes aspect from existence
- **Growth arc:** Learning constructive destruction; avoiding self-destruction
- **Quest focus:** Eliminating corruption or problems by destroying their aspect

### Bard
*One who allows destruction through [Aspect] or invites destruction of [Aspect].*

- **Role:** Wild card, chaos agent, passive destroyer
- **Power style:** Unintentional destruction; aspect works through them unpredictably
- **Growth arc:** Finding purpose in chaos; learning their role in the greater plan
- **Quest focus:** Causing necessary destruction through seeming incompetence or chaos

### Thief
*One who steals [Aspect] or steals through [Aspect].*

- **Role:** Taker, one who claims aspect for themselves at others' expense
- **Power style:** Direct theft; takes aspect from others or environment
- **Growth arc:** Learning to share; understanding consequences of selfishness
- **Quest focus:** Taking what they need to succeed, learning balance

### Rogue
*One who redistributes [Aspect] or redistributes through [Aspect].*

- **Role:** Robin Hood figure, redistributor
- **Power style:** Takes from those who have too much, gives to those who need
- **Growth arc:** Learning to accept help; believing in their own worth
- **Quest focus:** Balancing resources and helping the party succeed together

## Aspects

Aspects are the fundamental forces or concepts that players embody and manipulate. Each Aspect represents a duality or spectrum, and paired Aspects share thematic opposition.

### Time
*Represents: Progression, rhythm, decay, inevitability, mortality*

- **Powers:** Time travel, temporal loops, speeding/slowing time, seeing timelines, aging/de-aging
- **Themes:** Mortality, deadlines, cycles, patience, efficiency
- **Opposite:** Space

### Space
*Represents: Creation, size, dimension, physical reality, potential*

- **Powers:** Teleportation, resizing, dimensional manipulation, creation of matter, breeding the Genesis Frog
- **Themes:** Creativity, vastness, physical form, nurturing, patience
- **Opposite:** Time
- **Special:** Space players are critical for universe creation (Genesis Frog breeding)

### Light
*Represents: Knowledge, fortune, attention, relevance, publicity*

- **Powers:** Information gathering, luck manipulation, making things relevant, illumination
- **Themes:** Spotlight, knowing, visibility, significance, ego
- **Opposite:** Void

### Void
*Represents: Nothingness, secrets, irrelevance, obfuscation, the unknown*

- **Powers:** Invisibility, creating voids, hiding information, making things irrelevant
- **Themes:** Emptiness, mystery, insignificance, humility, the unknowable
- **Opposite:** Light

### Life
*Represents: Vitality, optimism, growth, healing, luxury*

- **Powers:** Healing, resurrection, vitality boosting, plant growth, life force manipulation
- **Themes:** Energy, positivity, indulgence, persistence, excess
- **Opposite:** Doom

### Doom
*Represents: Inevitability, suffering, judgment, fate, constraint*

- **Powers:** Predicting doom, inflicting suffering, accepting fate, death-related abilities
- **Themes:** Sacrifice, burden, rules, acceptance, pessimism

### Mind
*Represents: Logic, decisions, thought, karma, justice*

- **Powers:** Mind reading, decision manipulation, creating thought constructs, karmic justice
- **Themes:** Choices, rationality, consequences, fairness, intellect
- **Opposite:** Heart

### Heart
*Represents: Soul, emotion, identity, instinct, passion*

- **Powers:** Soul manipulation, inspiring emotions, identity shifting, passion amplification
- **Themes:** Feelings, self, desire, relationships, authenticity
- **Opposite:** Mind

### Hope
*Represents: Belief, imagination, faith, possibility, angels*

- **Powers:** Making beliefs real, inspiring hope, breaking rules through conviction, miracles
- **Themes:** Optimism, naivety, righteousness, dreams, idealism
- **Opposite:** Rage

### Rage
*Represents: Anger, skepticism, negative truth, destruction, demons*

- **Powers:** Inducing rage, seeing through lies, destroying falsehoods, fear manipulation
- **Themes:** Doubt, cynicism, chaos, rebellion, harsh truth
- **Opposite:** Hope

### Breath
*Represents: Freedom, direction, wind, detachment, flexibility*

- **Powers:** Wind manipulation, flight, direction changing, freeing from constraints

- **Opposite:** Breath

## Land Generation

Each player enters a unique Land upon starting the game. The Land reflects their personality, challenges they must overcome, and their role in the session.

### Land Naming Formula

Lands follow the pattern: **"Land of [Adjective/Noun] and [Noun]"** (abbreviated as LO[A]A[N], e.g., LOWAS, LOLAR).

**Generation rules:**

- **First element:** Usually relates to the physical environment (Wind, Pulse, Crypts, Frost, Heat, Light)
- **Second element:** Often relates to the quest or emotional theme (Shade, Wrath, Clockwork, Reflection, Tombs)
- Both elements should thematically connect to the player's Aspect, Class, or personality
- Avoid literal aspect names (e.g., for Time player use 'Pulse' or 'Clockwork', not 'Time')

### Land Components

Each Land must include:

- **Physical Environment:** Biome type (desert, ocean, crystal formations, mechanical structures, etc.)
- **Consort Species:** Simple creatures who provide advice and minor quests (salamanders, turtles, crocodiles, nakodiles, iguanas, etc.)
- **Central Problem:** The crisis affecting the Land that must be resolved (eternal darkness, time stopped, corruption spreading, etc.)
- **Denizen:** Powerful boss creature tied to the Land's theme and the player's quest (Typheus, Yaldabaoth, Cetus, Hephaestus, etc.)
- **Quest Arc:** Personal journey the player must complete, tied to their growth

### Environmental Palette

When generating Land descriptions, focus on:

- Dominant colors (tied to Aspect: Time=red, Space=white/green, Light=gold, etc.)
- Primary terrain features
- Atmosphere and weather
- Key landmarks (towers, ruins, natural formations)
- How the environment reflects the Land's problem

## Sprites and Prototyping

Sprites are magical kernelsprite companions that guide players and can be prototyped by throwing objects or creatures into them.

### Sprite Rules

- **Pre-entry prototyping:** Can be prototyped once before player enters; object remains in real world
- **Post-entry prototyping:** Can be prototyped once after player enters; object is consumed
- **Total prototypings:** Maximum of 2 prototypings per sprite
- **Enemy effect:** Each pre-entry prototyping adds that object's properties to all underlings in the session
- **Personality:** Sprites gain personality from prototyped entities; often cryptic but helpful
- **Knowledge:** Sprites have game knowledge but speak in riddles and metaphors

### Common Prototyping Types

- **Dead guardians/pets:** Grants knowledge and personality
- **Stuffed animals:** Grants personality quirks and appearance
- **Inanimate objects:** Grants abilities related to object
- **Multiple entities:** Sprite becomes a fusion of both prototypings

## Quest Structure

Players have multiple overlapping quest lines that must be completed to achieve God Tier and win the game.

### Personal Quest

A journey of character growth specific to the player's personality flaws and strengths. This quest challenges their worldview and pushes them to grow.

**Structure:**

1. Identify the player's central flaw or strength
2. Present situations that challenge this trait
3. Require the player to overcome or embrace this trait
4. Culminate in a choice that defines their character

### Land Quest

Solving the central problem of the Land, usually by defeating or negotiating with the Denizen.

**Typical structure:**

1. Discover the nature of the Land's crisis from consorts
2. Complete tasks to access deeper areas of the Land
3. Gather information about the Denizen
4. Confront the Denizen (combat or negotiation)
5. Resolve the Land's problem

### God Tier Quest

Achieving God Tier requires dying on the Quest Bed (Quest Cocoon) located at the heart of the player's Land.

**Requirements:**

- Find the Quest Bed (usually in a difficult-to-reach location)
- Die on the Quest Bed (must be a meaningful death, not random)
- The death must be either Heroic (saving others) or Just (accepting consequences)
- Upon resurrection, gain God Tier powers: conditional immortality, flight, class-specific abilities

### Session Quest

The collaborative goal of creating a new universe by breeding the Genesis Frog (requires a Space player) and defeating the Black King.

**Major milestones:**

1. All players enter the Medium successfully
2. Defeat underlings and climb the Echeladder
3. Space player breeds Genesis Frog with perfect genetic code
4. Light the Forge to allow passage to Skaia
5. Defeat the Black King on the Battlefield of Skaia
6. Create the new universe

The choice should always relate to the player's personal quest and force them to confront their character arc.

## Combat and Progression

### The Echeladder

Players progress by climbing rungs on the Echeladder, a level-up system with creative rung names.

**Mechanics:**

- Each rung grants increased stats, new abilities, or expanded inventory
- Rungs have thematically appropriate names (e.g., 'Gardenhoser', 'Tableflipper', 'Ectobiolobabysitter')
- Higher rungs require exponentially more experience
- Reaching certain milestone rungs unlocks special benefits

### Grist Types

Grist is the resource obtained from defeated enemies, used for alchemy and building.

| Grist Type | Usage |
|------------|-------|
| **Build Grist** | Most common; used for construction and basic alchemy |
| **Aspect Grist** | Named after aspects (Amber, Ruby, etc.); used for powerful alchemy |
| **Artifact Grist** | Rare grist from powerful enemies; needed for legendary items |
| **Zillium** | Extremely rare; required for ultimate creations |

### Underlings

Enemies in Sburb, influenced by sprite prototyping. Types include:

- **Imps:** Weakest enemies, most common
- **Ogres:** Mid-tier enemies, tougher variants
- **Basilisks:** Strong enemies with special abilities
- **Liches:** Powerful spellcaster enemies
- **Giclops:** Giant enemies with massive power

All underlings inherit traits from prototyped entities, making them unique to each session.

## Alchemy System

The alchemy system allows players to combine items to create new, often absurd, but powerful equipment.

### Core Mechanics

- Items have codes that can be combined using boolean operations (AND, OR, XOR)
- The Alchemiter creates new items based on combined codes
- Cost scales with item power (grist requirements increase exponentially)
- Players can create weapons, armor, tools, and utility items

### Combination Modes

- **AND (&&):** Creates fusion of both items' properties
- **OR (||):** Creates item with either set of properties
- **XOR:** Creates item excluding shared properties (rare/experimental)

### Generation Guidelines

When players attempt alchemy:

- Combine the concepts logically (hammer + sword = war hammer with sword edge)
- Name should portmanteau or reference both items
- Power scales with component rarity and grist cost
- Embrace absurdity but maintain internal logic
- More complex combinations = higher grist cost

## Game Systems

### Strife Specibus

The Strife Specibus determines what kind of weapons a player can equip. Once allocated, it's difficult (but not impossible) to change.

**Common types:**

- **Bladekind:** Swords, knives, daggers
- **Hammerkind:** Hammers, mallets, clubs
- **Riflekind:** Guns, rifles, firearms
- **Needlekind:** Needles, knitting needles, syringes
- **Spearkind:** Spears, lances, polearms
- **2xBladekind:** Dual-wielding variant
- **Fistkind:** Bare fists, gauntlets, brass knuckles
- Players can create absurd categories (Umbrellakind, Paperkind, etc.)

### Sylladex

The Sylladex is an inventory system with different Fetch Modus cards that determine how items are stored and retrieved.

**Common modi:**

- **Stack Modus:** Last in, first out (LIFO)
- **Queue Modus:** First in, first out (FIFO)
- **Array Modus:** Simple grid access
- **Tree Modus:** Hierarchical organization
- **Hashmap Modus:** Requires correct keyword
- **Memory Modus:** Must remember items exactly
- **Pictionary Modus:** Must draw item to retrieve
- **Wallet Modus:** Items must fit thematically

Modi can make inventory management challenging and comedic. Players can upgrade to better modi as they progress.

## Fraymotifs

Fraymotifs are powerful special attacks that players unlock as they master their class and aspect. They are devastating abilities with evocative names.

### Generation Rules

- Names should be dramatic and capitalize key words (e.g., 'Zillyhoo Hammer', 'Ancestral Awakening')
- Effects should reflect both Class and Aspect
- More powerful fraymotifs require higher levels
- Cooperative fraymotifs can be performed by multiple players

**Examples:** A Knight of Time might have 'Temporal Slash' (attack that rewinds enemy), while a Seer of Light might have 'Fortune's Revelation' (reveals enemy weaknesses).

## Game Manager Instructions

### Character Creation Process

When creating a new character:

1. Ask player about personality, interests, flaws, and goals
2. Assign or let player choose Class and Aspect (or use random if preferred)
3. Generate Land based on personality and aspect (use naming formula)
4. Assign appropriate Denizen based on aspect
5. Determine starting Strife Specibus and Fetch Modus
6. Outline personal quest arc (internal conflict to overcome)
7. Plan Land quest structure

### Dynamic Content Generation

When responding to player actions:

- Always respect established character traits and progression
- Generate alchemy results based on logical item fusion
- Scale challenges appropriately to player level
- Provide environmental descriptions that reflect Land themes
- Maintain consistency with sprite personalities
- Reference and build on previous events

### Narrative Principles

- Embrace Homestuck's tonal balance of humor and drama
- Use callbacks and recurring jokes appropriately
- Allow for player creativity and unexpected solutions
- Make failures interesting and drive plot forward
- Build toward satisfying payoffs for character arcs
- Create opportunities for collaboration between players

### Combat Guidelines

- Describe combat cinematically but concisely
- Account for weapon properties and alchemy effects
- Award appropriate grist for victories
- Progress Echeladder at dramatically appropriate moments
- Incorporate class abilities naturally
- Make underling variations reflect prototypings

## Appendix: Quick Reference

### Class-Aspect Pairings

Any Class can pair with any Aspect (144 combinations total). Consider thematic resonance:

- **Knight of Time:** Weaponizes time, protects timelines
- **Seer of Light:** Sees fortunate outcomes and important information
- **Heir of Breath:** Becomes wind, surrounded by freedom
- **Witch of Space:** Manipulates physical reality and size
- **Mage of Doom:** Knows suffering and fate intimately

### Session Progression Checklist

- [ ] All players enter the Medium
- [ ] Prototype sprites (pre-entry)
- [ ] Establish Land quests
- [ ] Begin alchemizing weapons and equipment
- [ ] Climb Echeladder through combat
- [ ] Find Quest Beds
- [ ] Complete personal quests
- [ ] Confront Denizens
- [ ] Achieve God Tier
- [ ] Breed Genesis Frog (Space player)
- [ ] Light the Forge
- [ ] Defeat Black King
- [ ] Create new universe

### Tone and Style Guide

Maintain Homestuck's characteristic voice:

- Mix absurdist humor with genuine emotional stakes
- Use creative capitalization for important concepts
- Embrace elaborate compound words and ridiculous item names
- Balance detailed description with narrative momentum
- Reference gamer culture and internet humor where appropriate
- Take character growth seriously even in comedic contexts

## Conclusion

This document provides the framework for running Sburb RPG sessions. The Game Manager should use these guidelines to generate consistent, engaging content while remaining flexible to player creativity. Remember that the core of Homestuck is about personal growth through absurd circumstances – balance the ridiculous with the meaningful, and always keep the focus on the players' journeys.

The game is won not just by defeating the Black King, but by each player fully realizing their role and growing into their mythological identity. Guide them toward this triumph through challenge, humor, and heart.
